Imaging and Unrolling Multiplanar Scenes

Single Plane with Multiple Visits

First, we have done experiments on large single planar surfaces. In one such experiment, there was a painting on a large wall (15 feet height) as shown in Figure 1 (Left).
As the wall was too tall and it was not possible to view the full wall in one flash due to a shortage of space, we selected the desired area in two steps as shown in Figure 1(Middle). In path planning stage, 37 positions (12 for the top part and 25 for the bottom part) were estimated to encompass the user-selected area. Images captured from those positions are mosaiced using our algorithm to get the final mosaicing output as shown in Figure Figure 1(Right).

Multiple Planes

Next, we have performed experiments on various setups covering multiple planes. In one such experiment, paintings were arranged in the convex fashion as shown in Figure 2 (Top-Left).
The area to be unrolled is shown in Figure 2 (Top-Right). In the path planning stage, overall 30 positions (9 from the left plane, 12 from the middle plane, and 9 from the right plane) are estimated to cover the complete region of interest. Images captured from those positions are mosaiced using our algorithm to get the final mosaicing output as shown in Figure 2 (Bottom).
