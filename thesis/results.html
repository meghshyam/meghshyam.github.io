Mosaicing Scenes With Vacant Spaces

The result of our algorithm on a scene containing vacant spaces is shown in Figure 1. In this experiment, the input stream had about 4300 images. The selection algorithm pruned the video into five images. A sample of the selected images are seen in Figure 1(Left). There were two disconnected components in the resulting graph. Adobe Photoshop was unable to produce any reasonable output as seen in Figure 1. The scene, captured from a distance is also shown. We see a better orthographic view of the posters in our result shown in Figure 1(right bottom).

Imaging Multiplanar Scenes

Our experiments are done on various setups covering multiple planes. In one such experiment, paintings were arranged in the convex fashion as shown in Figure 2 (Top-Left). We have selected the area to be imaged as shown in Figure 2 (Top-Right). In the path planning stage, overall 30 (9 from the left plane, 12 from the middle plane, and 9 from the right plane) positions to cover full region are estimated. Images captured from those positions are mosaiced using our algorithm to get the final mosaicing output as shown in the Figure 2 (Bottom).

Detecting Fiducials under Motion Blur

We put our fiducials on the quadcopter so that we can reliably recognize the quadcopters under motion. As seen in figure 3, we could detect the code embedded in our fiducial put on the quadcopter, in the presence of significant amount of motion blur.
